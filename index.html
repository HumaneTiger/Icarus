The story of Daedalus

and Icarus

Daedalus, the father of Icarus, was a great Athenian sculptor, craftsman and inventor in ancient Greece. After committing a dreadful crime, he was forced to flee from Athens to Crete, where he offered his services to King Minos, who was pleased to have such a skilled individual in his court.

Daedalus was not only responsible for building the impressive palace of Knossos, but also for conceiving and building the well-known labyrinth of Minos, where he imprisoned the centauroid Minotaur.

1/3

All these achievements did not prevent him and Icarus from being imprisoned in the highest tower of Crete, accused of conspiring against the king.

While observing the birds that circled his prison, the means for escape became clear. Daedalus asked his son to collect pieces of wood and built wooden structures that resembled bird wings. Using feathers from the flocks that perched on the tower, and the wax from candles, he had built two pairs of massive wings one for him and one for his son.

2/3

He strapped his wings on and noticed that his invention worked greatly. Then he helped Icarus to put on his wings and gave him a warning ...

And here is where our story starts.

You are Icarus. You are trying to fly away from your prison as only birds or gods could do.
